This page presents the final project for the PP434 course. The analysis focuses on Financial Empowerment and Economic Stability, utilizing advanced data visualization techniques to uncover policy insights.

Introduction

Financial Empowerment and Economic Stability

Financial literacy and inclusion are no longer just personal assets, they are essential components of a resilient global economy. This project investigates the current state of financial literacy and inclusion, exploring how these factors translate into real-world habits and macroeconomic stability. The goal of this project is to map the disparities in financial capabilities across countries and demographics to link their impact on economic behavior and inflation control.

Where are the gaps in financial literacy across the world?

Global Financial Literacy Benchmark

This map provides the baseline for our investigation by identifying regions with low literacy rates. It highlights that financial knowledge is not evenly distributed, which is the first step in understanding why some economies are more vulnerable than others.

Source: S&P Global Financial Literature Survey

Does greater financial inclusion mean saving more at the national level?

National Savings Capacity vs. Financial Inclusion

This chart explores the relationship between financial access and national saving rates. While countries with high GDP per capita generally show higher savings, the data reveals interesting exceptions.

This suggests that while inclusion is a tool, others factor as financial literacy, cultural factors and economic policy may also play a critical role in how much a nation actually saves.

Source: World Bank API

Which specific financial concepts are the hardest for people to understand?

Financial Knowledge by Country

This heatmap breaks down literacy into specific concepts. It shows that even the most of the countries, people struggle with "Compound Interest," helping us pinpoint exactly what should be taught in schools to improve economic behavior.

Source: OECD/INFE 2023 International Survey of Adult Financial Literacy

Are people receiving the same level of financial education?

Financial Knowledge by Socio-demographic Subgroups

This chart reveals that social inequality is a significant barrier to education. We observe a persistent gender gap in almost every country, but the data also shows a stark divide between income groups.

This suggests that policy work must focus on both empowering women and bridging the wealth-education gap to achieve true economic stability.

Source: OECD/INFE 2023 International Survey of Adult Financial Literacy

Do people actually practice what they know about money?

Financial Habits by Country

This map shows that "Comparing Products" and "Long-term Goals" are rare habits. It proves that education must focus on behavior to transform theoretical knowledge into real-world financial security.

Source: OECD/INFE 2023 International Survey of Adult Financial Literacy

Why do people in some regions still save "under the mattress"

Formal vs. Informal Savings by Demographic Groups

This comparison shows that in many emerging markets, informal saving remains more common than using banks. This highlights a lack of trust or physical access, which prevents household money from being protected by the safety and interest-earning potential of the formal financial system.

Source: World Bank, Global Findex Database

Does increasing the number of bank accounts actually help control inflation?

Regression Analysis: Financial Inclusion & Inflation

In my final analysis, I found a negative correlation: as financial inclusion increases, inflation stays lower. I argue that higher financial literacy could strengthen this effect; by promoting saving and efficient money management, literacy improves the transmission of monetary policy, making inflation control even more effective.

Source: World Bank API

Final conslusions

This data visualization project shows how financial literacy and access vary around the world. By transforming complex data into simple charts, patterns in money management can be identified across different groups. These visualizations serve as a starting point for discussing where financial authorities should focus their efforts.

While these charts reveal interesting links between financial literacy, financial inclusion, savings, and inflation, their aim is to spark curiosity rather than prove ultimate causes. This project encourages further research to explore the deeper reasons behind these trends. These visualizations can guide future studies to better understand the cultural and social factors that shape global economic stability.
